The Strategy

Three Moves That Changed Everything

RW identified the opportunity to build a full-stack cybersecurity platform in an underconsolidated market — then structured the three moves to make it happen.

01
Multi-Company Merger
Unified specialized cybersecurity firms — spanning managed security to threat intelligence — into a single, full-stack platform. Built the capability set no single firm could offer alone.
02
Cross-Border Expansion
Extended operations from Mexico into the United States, establishing commercial presence in multiple markets. Unlocked North American enterprise clients while preserving operational efficiency through a rightshore model.
03
Business Model Evolution
Transformed the revenue engine from project-based engagements to long-term managed services contracts — systematically building recurring, predictable income that commands premium valuations.

The Platform

Built to Scale Across Borders

Three structural innovations made the platform sustainable — not just large.

Rightshore Delivery Model
Latin American technical talent serving North American enterprise clients — delivering premium cybersecurity capabilities at competitive cost structures. The operational innovation behind the margin expansion.
Vendor-Agnostic Architecture
Built as a technology-neutral platform for enterprise clients in regulated industries. No vendor lock-in meant broader reach, faster sales cycles, and higher customer retention across sectors.
Private Equity Capital Secured
RW structured and closed a private equity investment that provided the capital required to accelerate geographic expansion and fund integration — without sacrificing strategic control.

The Transformation

From Projects to Platform

The business model shift was the engine of value creation — converting volatile, project-based income into a predictable, recurring revenue base that institutional investors prize.

Before
Project-Based Model
  • One-off engagements with limited continuity
  • Revenue difficult to forecast or model
  • High client acquisition cost per project
  • Limited platform leverage on costs
  • Valuation constrained by low predictability
After
Managed Services Platform
  • Long-term contracted managed services
  • Majority recurring revenue — contracted, predictable base
  • Expanding client relationships over time
  • Shared platform infrastructure — scalable margin
  • Premium valuation multiples at PE exit
